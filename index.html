Game Overview

"We thought it was abandoned. Offline. Dead. We were wrong."

The signal was faint — an automated distress beacon pulsing from deep within the drift. A derelict vessel, long thought lost, suddenly reappeared on long-range scans. No crew manifest. No flight logs. No response.

Your team of scavengers was dispatched to investigate. The mission was simple: board the ship, extract valuable tech, and return before the drift swallowed it again. But the moment you stepped aboard, the silence felt wrong. The air was stale, but not dead. The systems were dormant, but not cold. Something was watching.

Then your own ship powered down.

The Creature

The crew does not know what they are dealing with. They call it the Creature because they have no better word. It moves wrong. It appears where it should not. Weapons damage it but do not stop it. It is drawn to activity, to noise, to signal.

Creature States

StateDescription
DORMANTNot yet on board. Failed noise checks generate Threat Tokens only. Cannot activate until introduced.
ACTIVEOn the board. Full noise check rules apply. Position known to crew.
SIGNAL LOSTOn board but location unknown. Noise checks still fire. Position hidden until re-emergence.
VENTEDRetreated into ship infrastructure. Temporarily off board. Re-emerges via specific Echo cards.

Noise Check & Activation

// MechanicAt end of each player phase, roll D12 Noise Die vs accumulated Noise Level.
ResultOutcomeCreature Behavior
Roll > NoisePASSCreature moves HALF speed toward last noise location. Always moving — never fully still.
Fail · 1–4Direct PursuitFull speed directly toward last noise source.
Fail · 5–8Vent ShortcutUses speed to reach nearest vent (relative to creature) → emerges nearest vent to noise → continues with remaining speed.
Fail · 9–12Immediate VentInstant vent jump, no movement cost. Emerges at vent nearest noise source. Full speed toward noise.

Line of Sight Rush

After any movement resolves, if the creature ends with any player in direct line of sight it rushes immediately — ignoring remaining speed. Non-discriminating: attacks any player in its path, not just the target.

Introduction Sequence

  • Dormant Phase: Failed noise checks produce Threat Tokens. Toggle cards inert.
  • Bridge Objective: Crew reaches Bridge, interacts with main terminal → draws CONTACT card.
  • CONTACT Card: Names a room family. Placed pending on Task Board. Threat Tokens stack on it.
  • Introduction: Named room family discovered → creature placed in that room. Threat Tokens convert. Status flips to ACTIVE.
  • Scenario B: If family already on board when CONTACT drawn → creature emerges in most recently discovered room of that family.

The Echo Deck

The unified narrative engine. ~100 cards handling room discovery, loot, events, blueprints, mini-games, and data retrieval. Every draw carries potential to change board state, generate resources, trigger objectives, or interrupt play.

Deck Composition (~100 Cards)

Card TypeCountFunction
Room Cards~70–80Primary discovery loop. Room type, lore, resource icons, events, tasks, terminal indicators.
Echo Event Mini-Games10 (5×2)Interrupt mechanic. Must resolve before original draw continues.
Blueprint Cards~15–20%Unlock craftable items via Stacked Task chains.
CONTACT Card1Unique. Triggers creature introduction when drawn at Bridge terminal.

Drawing Rules

  • Room Discovery: Enter unexplored room → draw Echo Card to populate it.
  • Family Cap: Max 2–3 rooms per family on board simultaneously.
  • Cascade Draw: If drawn card's family is at cap → resolve card, draw again. Each cascade is another Echo Event risk.
  • Terminal Draw: Interacting with terminal costs 1–2 actions and draws an Echo Card.
  • Late Game: Once all rooms discovered, terminals are the only way to draw cards.

Bridge Terminals

One dedicated terminal per room family. Available regardless of whether that room exists on board.

// WarningBridge terminals generate significantly higher noise than standard terminals. Failed interaction triggers an alarm — immediate noise spike and noise check before turn ends.
// 05

Echo Event Mini-Games

Interrupt the current action — room discovery or terminal interaction — and must fully resolve before play continues. No additional action cost. After resolution, original action continues.

Universal Wager Rules

  • Mandatory: Every Echo Event requires staking one resource before any dice are rolled.
  • No Resources: Automatic failure. +4 Noise immediately. No rolls taken. Continue to next card draw.
  • Donation: Crewmate may cover wager if physically present in the same room when event triggers.
  • Win: Wagered resource returned. Some mini-games award doubled payout.
  • Fail: Resource permanently lost. Noise penalty applied per mini-game.

Data Retrieval Loop

  • Echo card drawn at terminal indicates a data location.
  • Place Data Token at indicated terminal or wall-mounted data point in any already-discovered room.
  • Navigate to that location. Must be adjacent to the terminal or data point.
  • Perform data extraction action to retrieve the token.

Preparation Actions

// Ready Action

  • Cost: 1 action. Requires weapon equipped.
  • Applies to ranged and melee equally.
  • Effect: +1 pushback beyond weapon's normal cap.
  • Expires at end of creature phase if unused.

// Brace Action

  • Cost: 1 action. No weapon required.
  • Roll all 5 D12 system dice once.
  • Each Hazard or Wild face = −1 damage. Maximum −5.
  • Stacks with Armor and Defense Roll.

Defense Roll

WeaponDice PoolMax Pushback
Unarmed1 die0 (no pushback)
1×1 (Pistol, Knife, etc.)2 dice2 spaces
1×2 (Rifle, Axe, etc.)3 dice3 spaces
1×3 (Shotgun)4 dice4 spaces
4-slot (Flamethrower)5 dice (max)5 spaces
  • Up to 3 rolls. Keep Hazard and Wild faces between rolls.
  • Void face permanently removes that die for the sequence.
  • Noise and Noise Spike faces ignored during combat.
  • Each Hazard or Wild face = −1 damage AND 1 pushback space.
  • No pass/fail — sliding scale only. Every face contributes.
  • Ready action adds +1 pushback on top of weapon cap.

Makeshift Armor

  • Passive and automatic. No action or roll required.
  • Mitigation = armor's inventory slot size.
  • Multiple pieces stack. Each destroyed after absorbing one hit.
  • Applied after all rolls resolved.

Full Encounter Sequence

StepAction
1Apply Brace faces (if Brace taken). Note Ready bonus.
2Defense Roll: weapon dice pool, up to 3 rolls, keep Hazard/Wild, Void removes die.
3Damage: 5 base − Brace faces − Defense faces − Armor. Floors at 0.
4Pushback: 1 space per face, capped at pool size + Ready bonus.
5Melee check: flip token (or destroy). Grant +1 bonus Action.
6Player Flee: all players, all encounter types. Full speed, 0 noise, creature does not pursue.
// Melee BonusMelee weapon users receive +1 Action on top of the standard flee — rewarding players who choose to fight back.

Inventory & Equipment

Player Board Layout

  • Top: Character name, art, vital stats (Speed, Health tracker).
  • Left column: Equipment Grid (2×4 = 8 slots) — weapons and armor only.
  • Right column: Backpack Inventory Grid (~22 slots with compartments).
  • Bottom: Noise Tracker (1–12) and Quick Reference section.

Equipment Grid

  • 2×4 grid (8 slots). Melee weapons and Makeshift Armor only.
  • Ranged weapons stored in backpack, competing with resources.
  • Starting 1×1 weapon placed here at setup.

Backpack Inventory

  • ~22 slots in compartments. Compartments are cosmetic — items go anywhere they fit.
  • Certain compartments have designated item types (e.g., Fuel Cell slot).
  • Reserved Slots: Can hold any item when empty. Designated item takes priority when needed — other items must relocate or be dropped.
  • Backpack boards are interchangeable between playthroughs.

Fixed Board Elements

  • The Bridge: ~60 spaces. Fixed position every game. Multiple terminals, Captain's section, one family terminal per room type. Higher noise, alarm risk.
  • The Dock: Fixed on board edge. Escape ship berth. Houses 2×10 Fuel Grid.
  • Corridors: Fixed, various shapes. No Echo cards. Vent access points printed here. Board designed so no sections can be sealed off by tile placement.
  • Vent Network: Printed on board in space between room/corridor boundaries. Traversable. 1–2 spaces wide. Creature-native but player-accessible.

Room Tiles

  • 5×6 footprint with asymmetrical notched corners.
  • Double-sided. Only one face physically fits a given board slot.
  • Player chooses one of two orientations — 4 total configurations per tile.
  • Door routing determines which fixed corridors connect to the room.

Hiding

Locker Hiding

  • Enter: 1 action. Must be adjacent to locker. +1 noise.
  • Exit: FREE at start of turn, before movement. +1 noise.
  • Creature Adjacent: Low-Risk roll to avoid detection. Roll mechanic TBD.

Vent Hiding

Players may hide within the vent network. Noise marker position determines viability — if last noise location points toward player's vent section, creature may already be heading there. Full rules TBD.

Additional Locations

Two additional hiding location types planned: debris piles (medium risk), maintenance crawlspaces, and bulkhead alcoves (high risk). Risk tiers and detection rolls TBD.

The Escape Phase

  • Fuel Grid: 2×10 grid at the Dock. 20 total spaces. Need 16 filled.
  • Official Cell: 2×2 (4 spaces). Found via Task Board chains.
  • Jury-Rigged Cell: 1×2 (2 spaces). Craftable. Twice the trips.
  • Carrier Rule: Fuel Cell carrier must drop heavy weapons or armor. Crew acts as bodyguards.
  • Death Drop: Carrier death drops cell at exact location. Recovery mission required.

Combat Specialist Abilities

// Hardened (Passive)

After all 3 Defense Roll attempts are exhausted, may reroll 1 die one additional time. Defense rolls only. Once per creature encounter.

// Force (Passive)

Pushback is not capped at weapon's dice pool size. Automatically gains +1 pushback beyond normal cap on every Defense Roll. Applies to all weapons.

Task Board

Official term. Previously referred to as Objective Board — retired. Echo cards that cannot be immediately resolved are placed here as pending tasks.

Task Types

  • Singular Tasks: Single Echo card. Resolved when crew completes the indicated objective. Removed when fulfilled.
  • Stacked / Chained / Nested Tasks: One card requires 2+ prerequisite cards completed first. Cards placed alongside parent card. Resolved bottom-up.
  • Blueprint Tasks: Blueprint Echo cards use Stacked Task system as mini quest chains to unlock craftable items for the whole crew.
